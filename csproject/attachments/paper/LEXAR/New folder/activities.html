Activities

Go for a swim

Swimming, especially in natural lakes can be extremely relaxing. But even if you don’t have a lake nearby and your only option is chlorine treated water, it can still give you all the benefits. Play around in the water, float in it, feel the water and listen to the water as you swim. You can also use a flotation product like an air mat or pool tube and enjoy a relaxing float in the water.

Get yourself a massage

A good massage can not only help you relax but also help reduce anxiety, digestive disorders, headaches, sleep issues and joint pains. You can go to a massage parlor or even better ask your partner to give you a full body massage. Remember to return the favor! You can also give yourself a self massage if no one else is around. There is a practice of self massage called Abhyanga in Ayurveda which can be highly relaxing. Here’s how to do Abhyanga.

Sing or dance

Put on your favorite chill out music and move your body to the rhythm. Don’t focus on how you are dancing. Instead, let go, lose yourself to the rhythm and let the rhythm guide you. Moving your body this way helps deeply relax your mind and body.

Watch your childhood cartoons

Make a list of all the cartoons you used to love watching as child and watch them on Netflix. Try connecting with your inner child as you do this. Cartoons like Winnie the Pooh, The Poddington Peas, Arthur, The Wild Thornberrys, As Told By Ginger, Lilo and Stitch, The Flintstones and many others are not only fun to watch, but also have a relaxing vibe to them. ‘Winnie the Pooh’, definitely tops the list. You might even find many of these cartoons on youtube.

Go on a long drive

Find a scenic route and go on a long drive. Roll the windows down and breath in the fresh air whilst admiring the nature around you.
